Dejected, is this how the days would pass?

Posted: 2020-12-20   |   Categories: poetry   |   Tags: eliya, jaun, poetry, shayari

Here is a ghazal by the Urdu poet Jaun Eliya.

  1. बे-दिली क्या यूँही दिन गुज़र जाएँगे

       सिर्फ़ ज़िंदा रहे हम तो मर जाएँगे

  1. रक़्स है रंग पर रंग हम-रक़्स हैं

       सब बिछड़ जाएँगे सब बिखर जाएँगे

  1. ये ख़राबातियान-ए-ख़िरद-बाख़्ता

       सुब्ह होते ही सब काम पर जाएँगे

  1. कितनी दिलकश हो तुम कितना दिल-जू हूँ मैं

       क्या सितम है कि हम लोग मर जाएँगे

  1. है ग़नीमत कि असरार-ए-हस्ती से हम

       बे-ख़बर आए हैं बे-ख़बर जाएँगे

I have been thinking about these lines for a while now. I have written some of my thoughts below. The enumeration below is with respect to the couplet/sher indexing followed above.

Here is an English translation that I have written.

  1. Dejected, is this how the days would pass?

       If I just remain alive, death would follow.

  1. Dance is the ambience, but the mood longs for a partner

       We all would be seperated and broken apart.

  1. All of this drinking, putting the intellect at stake

       The next day all of us would go to work.

  1. So irresistible you are, so heartbroken I am,

       What a tragedy it is that we are mortals.

  1. What we may take away is that the secrets of existence,

       were never revealed to us, and will never be.

Analysis

Jaun Eliya has a style of using the same word in the same line, either in different meanings and context or just to stress the point.
